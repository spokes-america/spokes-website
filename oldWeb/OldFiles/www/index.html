For the summer of 2017, a team of 8 MIT and Harvard students will bike across the United States in collaboration with edX and Teach for America as part of an effort to rethink STEM education. As we go, we will stop at a dozen schools, libraries, and camps throughout the country to hold learning festivals. At each school, we will teach hands-on, project-based classes based on our passions in STEM.

This Year's Workshops

Model rockets: mechanical engineering!

Outer space is pretty far away. So how do we get there? In this workshop, students will explore basic model rocketry to begin to understand the physical limitations on flight, aerodynamics, and propulsion. By building and launching their own model rockets, students will also learn how different design decisions can drastically change the outcome of their product.

Scratch: computer science!

In the world today, programming has become almost as vital a skill as reading and writing. Companies these days look for all of their future employees to have a basic knowledge of computer languages. But to learn a computer language you need a certain way of thinking. In this workshop students will learn the important skills of how to think procedurally, which is the first step to becoming a computer programmer. Once theyâ€™ve had a chance to explore this new concept, they will implement it with the online programming tool Scratch. After the workshop students will have the basic skills necessary to learn any programming language they wish to pursue.

How our brains work: Neuroscience!

Our brains work behind the scenes to shape the way we experience the world. In this workshop, students will explore the cellular basis for how our brains work, measure the electrical activity in their own muscles, learn about perception and the part our brains play in "the dress" phenomenon, and more. Students will leave with an understanding and appreciation for the wacky way our minds operate.

Math can be fun!

Math helps us understand the world as we know it. Students will explore real-life probability and see how it comes into play with sports and Deal or No Deal. Additionally, students will learn mental math techniques and basic graph theory.

Our Motivation

We are dedicated to revealing the exploratory, self-directed, and boundless nature of learning to students across the US. Our mission stems from the simple idea that most of the learning we do over the course of our lifetimes happens outside of a classroom as the result of semi-random explorations into topics that genuinely interest us.

We want to show this to high school students and give them an opportunity to feel inspired and find something they love.
